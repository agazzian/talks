Random splitting of fluid models

A. Agazzi, J. Mattingly, O. Melikechi

Risorgimento della vita felice, ETHZ, 05.07.2022

Main Motivation

Turbulence in fluid dynamics

$\bullet$ The periodicity of the orbits of $\Phi_t(q)$ and the shearing result in $$D\Phi_{t_n}(q) = I + n A$$ and the stretching of a tangent vector contradicts the existence of a metric wrt which $\Phi$ is conformal

$\bullet$ The weak bracket condition in $TT\mathcal M$ guarantees that the maps connecting $q$ to $q'$ has a density in $T_{q'}\mathcal M$, so the map does not preserve subspaces of the tangent bundle.

Lorenz 96

A toy model for numerical weather prediction (Lorentz, '96):

$$ \frac d{dt} x_{k}(t) = x_{k-1}(t) (x_{k+1}(t) - x_{k-2}(t)) \color{red}{- x_{k}(t) + F}\qquad \text{for } k \in \{1,\dots, n\}\label{e:alg} $$

where $x_{-1} = x_{n-1}$, $x_0 = x_n$ and $x_{n+1} = x_{1}$

The conservative dynamics leave the energy $\|x\|_2^2$ invariant (verify: $\dot x \cdot x = 0$).

This model is known to display bifurcations as $F$ increases.

2d Navier-Stokes

Navier Stokes equs modeling the evolution of the velocity field $u=u(x,t)$

$$ \begin{cases} \partial_t u + (u \cdot \nabla) u = \color{red}{\nu \Delta u} - \nabla p \color{red}{+ F}\\ \mathrm{div}(u) = \nabla\cdot u = 0 \end{cases} $$

for $x \in [0,2\pi]^2$, where $\nu>0$ controls dissipaton and $p$ is the pressure

The evolution of the vorticity $q=q(x,t):=\mathrm{curl}(u)$ reads

\begin{align} \partial_t q + (\mathcal K q \cdot \nabla) q = \color{red}{\nu \Delta q + f} \end{align}

where $\mathcal K$ is the Biot-Savart kernel

Random forcing

Stochastically forced versions of such systems were extensively studied:

$$ \frac d{dt} x_{k}(t) = x_{k-1}(t) (x_{k+1}(t) - x_{k-2}(t)) \color{red}{- x_{k}(t) + F} \color{blue}{+ \dot W_k} $$

$$ \begin{align} \partial_t q(x,t) + (\mathcal K q(x,t) \cdot \nabla) q(x,t) = \color{red}{\nu \Delta q(x,t) + f(x)} \color{blue}{+ \sum_k \sigma_k(x)~ \dot W_k} \end{align} $$

[Flandoli et al. 95+...], [MattinglyE00], [FlandoliRomito02+08+...], [Romito04+08+...], [MattinglyPardoux05], [HairerMattingly06+08+...], [DebusscheRomito14], ...

The "effect" of noise in these analyses is multifold: it acts both on

  • the conservative dynamics of the system (regularization, smoothing, ...)
  • the forced/dissipative dynamics of the system (energy exchange)

Split dynamics

We can decompose a vector field as $ V(x) = \sum_{k=1}^n V_k(x) $

and approximately recover the flow map $\varphi$ solving $\dot \varphi_t(x) = V(\varphi_t(x))$ combining the flow maps $\varphi_.^{(k)}$ of $V_k$: for small $h$ we expect that

$$ \varphi_{ h}(x) \approx \varphi_{h}^{(n)}\circ\dots \circ \varphi_{h}^{(1)}(x) $$

$$ \varphi_{t}(x) \approx \left(\varphi_{h}^{(n)}\circ\dots \circ \varphi_{h}^{(1)}\right)^m(x) \quad \text{for } t=mh $$

We add randomness by randomizing the flow times: for $\tau_i \stackrel{iid}{\sim} \text{Exp}(h)$ $$ \Phi_{\tau}(x) := \varphi_{\tau_n}^{(n)}\circ\dots \circ \varphi_{\tau_1}^{(1)} [\color{red}{\circ \varphi_{\tau_0}^{(0)}}](x) \approx \varphi_{nh}(x) $$ where $\tau \in \mathbb R^n$ is a random vector throughout

Split Lorenz 96 dynamics

We can split the Lorenz 96 dynamics (for $k \in \{1,\dots, N\}$)

$$ \begin{align*} \dot x_{k}(t) &= \color{green}{x_{k-1}(t)}~ ( \color{green}{x_{k+1}(t)} - x_{k-2}(t)) \,\color{red}{- x_{k}(t) + F}\\ \dot x_{k+1}(t) &= \color{green}{x_{k}(t)} \quad(x_{k+2}(t) \color{green}{- x_{k-1}(t)}) \,\color{red}{- x_{k+1}(t) + F} \end{align*} $$

by defining the vector fields

$$ \begin{align*} & \color{green}{V_k(x) = x_{k-1} (x_{k+1}e_k - x_{k}e_{k+1})}\qquad \\ &\color{red}{V_0(x) =- x + F} \end{align*} $$

These dynamics conserve the energy $\|x\|_2^2$ (verify: $\dot x \cdot x = 0$).

Split Galerkin Navier Stokes dynamics

For $j \in \mathbb Z^2$ the (complex) Fourier modes $q_j = a_j + i b_j \in \mathbb C$ interact in triples $j,k,\ell \in \mathbb Z^2$ satisfying $j+k+\ell = 0$ according to

$$ \begin{align*}{\large V_{jk\ell}^{(1-4)}~:}\quad \begin{cases} \dot{a}_j = C_{k\ell}a_ka_\ell \\ \dot{a}_k = C_{j\ell}a_ja_\ell \\ \dot{a}_\ell = -C_{jk}a_ja_k \end{cases}\quad \begin{cases} \dot{a}_j = C_{k\ell}b_kb_\ell \\ \dot{b}_k = C_{j\ell}a_jb_\ell \\ \dot{b}_\ell = -C_{jk}a_jb_k \end{cases}\quad \begin{cases} \dot{b}_j = C_{k\ell}a_kb_\ell \\ \dot{a}_k = C_{j\ell}b_jb_\ell \\ \dot{b}_\ell = -C_{jk}b_ja_k \end{cases}\quad \begin{cases} \dot{b}_j = -C_{k\ell}b_ka_\ell \\ \dot{b}_k = -C_{j\ell}b_ja_\ell \\ \dot{a}_\ell = C_{jk}b_jb_k. \end{cases}\qquad \end{align*} $$

where $ C_{jk} = \frac{\langle j^\perp, k\rangle}{4 \pi} \left(\frac 1 {|k|^2}-\frac 1 {|j|^2}\right)$

The dynamics conserve the enstrophy $\mathcal E_0 (q) = \|q\|_2^2 = \sum_{j=1}^{(2N+1)^2-1} |q_j|^2$ $\qquad \qquad \qquad \quad $ and the energy $\mathcal E_1 (q) = \|q\|_{-1}^2= \sum_{j=1}^{(2N+1)^2-1} \frac{|q_j|^2}{|j|^2}$

Convergence results

Let $S_t g(x) := g(\varphi_t(x))$, $\widetilde S_h g(x) := g(\Phi_\tau(x))$ and $P_h g := \mathbb E[\widetilde S_h g]$ then

Theorem (A., Mattingly, Melikechi, 2022):

Let $\Phi_\tau$ be the random flow map for either Lorenz 96 or galerkin Navier stokes dynamics. For any $t>0$ there exits $C>0$ such that, for $t=m h$ $$\|S_t - P_h^m \|_{2 \to 0}< C h $$ the trajectories of $\Phi_\tau^m(x)$ converge almost surely to the ones of $\varphi_t(x)$: $$\mathbb P[\limsup_{h\to 0} \|S_t - \widetilde S_{h}^{m}\|_{2 \to 0}> \epsilon] = 0$$

The operator norm is defined as $\|P\|_{2 \to 0}:=\sup_{g~:~\|g\|_2 = 1} \|P g\|_0$

Convergence: Sketch of proof

Let $\tau_i \sim \mathrm{Exp}(1)$, and recall that $\widetilde{S}_{h\tau}g(x) = g(\Phi_{h\tau}(x)) $, then we have

$$ \begin{align*} \partial_h\widetilde{S}_{h\tau} &=\sum_{k=1}^n \tau_k e^{h\tau_1V_1}\cdots e^{h\tau_{k-1}V_{k-1}}V_k e^{h\tau_kV_k}\cdots e^{h\tau_nV_n}\\ & = \sum_{k=1}^n \tau_kV_k\widetilde{S}_{h\tau}+\sum_{k=1}^n \tau_k[\widetilde{S}^{(1,k-1)}_{h\tau}, V_k]\widetilde{S}^{(k,n)}_{h\tau}= V\widetilde{S}_{h\tau}+(V_\tau-V)\widetilde{S}_{h\tau}+E_{h\tau} \end{align*} $$

by variation of constants then we have

$$ \begin{align} \widetilde{S}_{h\tau}-S_h = \int_0^h S_{h-r}(V_\tau-V)\widetilde{S}_{r\tau} dr+\int_0^hS_{h-r}E_{r\tau} dr. \end{align} $$

both remainders have order $\mathcal O(h^2)$, so $\|P_{h}-S_{h}\|_{2\to 0} \sim \mathcal O(h^2)$

Ergodicity: Lorenz 96

Let $\mathcal H = \{x \in \mathcal R^{n}~:~\sum_{k=1}^n (x_{k-1}^2+x_{k+1}^2)x_k^2\neq 0\}$

Theorem (A., Mattingly, Melikechi, 2022):

For the conservative Lorenz 96 randomly split dynamics ($V_1, \dots, V_n$), Lebesgue measure is the unique invariant measure for $P_h$ on $\mathcal H\cap \mathcal S^{n-1}$.

Theorem (A., Mattingly, Melikechi, 2022):

For the forced/dissipative randomly split Lorenz 96 ($\color{red}{V_0},V_1, \dots, V_n$), let the fixed point of $V_0$ satisfy $x^*=F/\nu\not \in \mathcal H$. Then $P_h$ has a unique invariant measure $\mu$ to which the system converges exponentially fast in a weighted total varation norm.

Ergodicity: Galerkin Navier Stokes

Let $\mathcal H = \{x \in \mathbb R^{n}~:~\exists \tau \in \mathbb R_+^{n},~\mathrm{supp}(\Phi_\tau(x))=\{1,\dots, n\}\}$

Theorem (A., Mattingly, Melikechi, 2022):

For the Euler randomly split dynamics ($V_1, \dots, V_n$), there exists at most one invariant measure for $P_h$ on $\mathcal H \cap \{q~:~\mathcal E_0(q) = \mathcal E_0, \mathcal E_1(q) = \mathcal E_1\}$.

Theorem (A., Mattingly, Melikechi, 2022):

For the randomly split Galerkin Navier Stokes ($\color{red}{V_0},V_1, \dots, V_n$), let the fixed point of $\color{red}{V_0}$ satisfy a weak bracket (or Hörmander) conditon. Then $P_h$ has a unique invariant measure $\mu$ to which the system converges exponentially fast in a weighted total varation norm.

Ergodicity: Galerkin Navier Stokes

Let $\mathcal H = \{x \in \mathbb R^{n}~:~\exists \tau \in \mathbb R_+^{n},~\mathrm{supp}(\Phi_\tau(x))=\{1,\dots, n\}\}$

A weak bracket condtion holds at $x$ if the set of vectors $$ \mathcal{V}_{m+1} = \left\{[V_k,W] : W\in\mathcal{V}_m, 0\leq k\leq n\right\}\cup\mathcal{V}_m. $$ spans the tangent space of the model at $x$ for $m$ large enough.

Theorem (A., Mattingly, Melikechi, 2022):

For the randomly split Galerkin Navier Stokes ($\color{red}{V_0},V_1, \dots, V_n$), let the fixed point of $\color{red}{V_0}$ satisfy a weak bracket (or Hörmander) conditon. Then $P_h$ has a unique invariant measure $\mu$ to which the system converges exponentially fast in a weighted total varation norm.

Ergodicity of gNSE: Sketch of proof

  • In the presence of the forcing term $V_0(q) = - \nu \Lambda q + F$ there exists $r>0$ with $\mathcal B_r(0)$ being forward invariant wrt the split dynamics. This implies tighness and existence follows by Krylov-Bogolubov.
  • Further, a Doeblin minorization condition holds: letting $P_h$ be the semigroup of a cycle, by the weak bracket condition there exists $m>0$ a set $B$ and a constant $c>0$ suc that for any $q_0 \in \mathcal B_r(0)$ $$ P_h^{m+1}(q_0, B) > c_h \lambda (\cdot \cap B) $$ Uniqueness and exponential convergence follow from the above by a standard coupling argument.

Lyapunov exponents

Let $P_t$ have a unique inv. meas. $\mu$, then (by the multiplicative ergodic theorem) there exists a deterministic $\lambda_1$ such that $\mathbb P\times \mu$-a.e. in $(\omega, x)$, $$ \lambda_1 := \lim_{t \to \infty} \frac 1 t \log |D \Phi_t(x)| $$ This describes asymptotic rate of separation of trajectores, and $\lambda_1>0$ hints for chaos

Theorem (A., Mattingly, Melikechi, 2022+):

Let $\Phi_\tau$ be the random flow map for the conservative Lorenz 96 or the Galerkin Euler dynamics. The Lyapunov exponent of $\Phi_\tau$ is strictly positive: $\lambda_1>0$.

Proof sketch: part 1

The proof hinges on the following result:

Theorem (Baxendale, 1983):

If sufficiently regular, conservative flow $\Phi_\tau$ ($\sum_{i} \lambda_i = 0$) has a positive Lyapunov exponent, then either
$\bullet$ There exists a metric $g_x$ on $\mathcal M$ such that $\Phi$ is conformal wrt $g_x$
$\bullet$ For every $x \in \mathcal M$ and $\tau$ there exists a nontrivial family of subspaces $T \in T_{x}\mathcal M$ that are left invariant by $\Phi_\tau$

Proof sketch: part 2

We disprove that (a) and (b) hold in our setting:

$\bullet$ The periodicity of the orbits of $\Phi_t(q)$ and the shearing result in $$D\Phi_{t_n}(q) = I + n A$$ and the stretching of a tangent vector contradicts the existence of a metric wrt which $\Phi$ is conformal

$\bullet$ The weak bracket condition in $TT\mathcal M$ guarantees that the maps connecting $q$ to $q'$ has a density in $T_{q'}\mathcal M$, so the map does not preserve subspaces of the tangent bundle.
