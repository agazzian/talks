Mass action kinetics

Consider the reaction \(A + B \overset{r_2}{\rightharpoonup} 2B \). We see sets of

\(\bullet\) Molecules \( \ \mathcal S = \{A,B\}\)
\(\bullet\) In/out combinations \( \mathcal C = \{c_{\rm in}^{r_2}, c_{\rm out}^{r_2}\} = \{(1,1),(0,2)\}\qquad\)

The vector of concentrations \(x(t) \in \mathbb R_+^2\) for \(A + B \rightharpoonup 2B\) obeys

$$\begin{cases}\dot x_A = - k \cdot x_A x_B \\ \dot x_B = k \cdot x_A x_B \end{cases}$$

Mass action kinetics

A chemical reaction network \((\mathcal S, \mathcal C, \mathcal R)\) is defined by the sets

\(\bullet \ \mathcal S := \{s_1, s_2,\dots, s_d\}\) of chemical species,
\(\bullet \ \mathcal R := \{r_1, r_2,\dots, r_m\}\) of chemical reactions,
\(\bullet \ \mathcal C := \bigcup_{r \in \mathcal R} \{c_{\rm in}^r, c_{\rm out}^r\}\) of reaction complexes.$\qquad\qquad\qquad$

Then, the vector of concentrations \(x(t) \in \mathbb R_+^d\) obeys a set of coupled ODEs

$$\frac{{\text d} x}{{\text d}t} = \sum_{r \in \mathcal R} \lambda_r(x)\, c^r \qquad {\rm with } \qquad \lambda_r(x) := k_r \prod_{i = 1}^d x_i^{(c_{\rm in}^r)_i}$$

where \( c^r = c_{\rm out}^r - c_{\rm in}^r \in \mathbb Z^d\) are reaction vectors and \(k_r > 0\) are reaction rate constants.

Deterministic dynamics

$$\emptyset \overset{r_1}{\rightharpoonup} A + B \overset{r_2}{\rightharpoonup} 2B \overset{r_3}{\rightharpoonup} A\\ \begin{cases}\dot x_A & = k_1 - k_2 x_A x_B + k_3 x_B^2\\ \dot x_B & = k_1 + k_2 x_A x_B - 2 k_3 x_B^2\end{cases}$$

Microscopic Markov model

With \(N_t \in \mathbb N_0^d\) counting the molecules of each species in a reactor of volume \(v\), we define the concentration vector \(X_t^v = v^{-1} N_t\in (v^{-1} \mathbb N_0)^d\) and model the dynamics of the system as a pure jump Markov model.

Each reaction \(r\) (a jump by \(v^{-1}c^r\)) occurs at a rate \(v \Lambda_{r}^{(v)}\) where

$$\Lambda_r^{(v)}(x):= \frac{k_r}{v^{\|c_{\rm in}^r\|_1}}\prod_{i = 1}^d\binom{v x_i}{(c_{\rm in}^r)_i}(c_{\rm in}^r)_i! = k_r \prod_{i = 1}^d x_i^{(c_{\rm in}^r)_i}+ o_v(1)$$

Generator of $X_t^v$: $\mathcal L_v f(x) := v\sum_{r \in \mathcal R} \Lambda_{r}^{(v)} (x) \left(f(x + v^{-1}c^r) -f(x)\right)$

Law of Large Numbers

Theorem $\left[\text{Kurtz '70}\right]$:

The sample paths of the process $X_t^v$ on \(t \in [0,T]\) converge almost surely for \(v \to \infty\) and \(X_0^v \to x_0\) to the deterministic solution \(x(t)\) with initial condition \(x(0) = x_0\).

$\emptyset \overset{r_1}{\rightharpoonup} A + B \overset{r_2}{\rightharpoonup} 2B \overset{r_3}{\rightharpoonup} A$

Large Deviations Principle (LDP)

A LDP holds if, for any set \(\Gamma \subset D_{0,T} (\mathbb R_+^d)\), \(X_0^v = x_0^v \to x_0\), we have

$$ \mathbb P_{x_0^v}\left[X^v \in \Gamma\right] \asymp\exp\left[-v \inf_{z \in \Gamma} I_{x_0,T}(z)\right]\qquad \text{as } v \to \infty$$

where we define the Lagrangian and the rate (action) functional as

$$\begin{align*} L( z, z') :=& \inf_{ \{\mu\in\mathbb R_+^{|\mathcal R|} :~ \sum_{r \in \mathcal R\\ \,} \mu_r c^r = z' \}}\!\,\,\, \sum_{r \in \mathcal R} \lambda_r(z) - \mu_r + \mu_r \log \frac {\mu_r}{\lambda_r(z)} \qquad \quad \\ I_{x_0,T}(z) := & \begin{cases}\int_0^T L(z(s),z'(s)) {\rm d}s &z \in \mathcal{AC}_{0,T}(\mathbb R_+^d), z(0) = x_0\\\infty & \text{otherwise}\end{cases} \end{align*} $$

 

Obstructions to LDP

Standard results establishing LDPs for Markov jump processes assume that the rates \(\Lambda_r^{(v)}(x)\) are bounded away from zero.

This is not true in our setting (boundary effects: \(\Lambda_r^{(v)}(0) = 0\)). However...

Example: Consider the reactions $r_1~:~A \to \emptyset$, $r_2~:~2A \to 3 A$, with

$$\lambda_1(x) = x\,,\qquad \lambda_2(x) = x^2 1_{[0,1]}(x)$$

We see that $X_0=0$ is an absorbing state for $X_t^v$. However for $z(t) = t$

$$I_{0,T}(z) \leq \int_0^1 1 \log \frac 1{\lambda_2(t)} -1 + \lambda_2(t)+ \lambda_1(t) \,d t < \infty $$

$\Rightarrow$ At least formally the LDP makes sense

Existing literature

Multiple efforts were made to relax this assumption:

  • Dupuis, Ramanan and Wu. Large deviation principle for finite-state mean field interacting particle systems. Technical Report 1601.06219, arXiv (2016).
  • A., Dembo and Eckmann. Large deviations theory for Markov jump models of chemical reaction networks. Annals of Applied Probability (2018)
  • Anderson, Cappelletti, Kim and Nguyen. Tier structure of strongly endotactic reaction networks. Stochastic Processes and their Applications (2020)
  • Patterson and Renger. Large Deviations of Jump Process Fluxes. Mathematical Physics, Analysis and Geometry 22 (2019)
  • Kratz and Pardoux. Large deviations for infectious diseases models, Séminaire de Probabilités XLIX. Lecture Notes in Mathematics, 2215 (2018)

The proof of LDP lower bound for $\mathbb P_{x_0^v}[\|X^v-z\| < \epsilon]$ follows 3 steps:

  • Split up the time interval $[0,T]$ into small intervals of length $\eta > 0$
  • In each interval, approximate the path $z$ as a linear path $\bar z^{(i)}$
  • show that the large deviations "cost" of the process $X_t^v$ following the path $\bar z_i$ is approximately given by $\mathbb P[\|X^v - \bar z^{(i)}\|< \epsilon] \asymp \exp\left[- v \eta L(z,\bar z^{(i)})\right]$ where
  • $$L(z,\bar z^{(i)}) = \sum_r \lambda_r(z) - \mu_r + \log\left( \frac{\lambda_r(z)}{\mu_r}\right) \qquad \text{for } \sum_{r} \mu_rc^r = (\bar z^{(i)})' $$ $$\begin{align*}\Rightarrow\mathbb P_{x_0^v}[\|X^v-z\| < \epsilon] \approx \prod_i \mathbb P[\|X^v-\bar z^{(i)}\| < \epsilon] &\asymp \exp\left[- v \eta \sum_i L(z,\bar z^{(i)})\right] \\&\xrightarrow{\eta \to 0} \exp\left[- v I_{x_0,T}(z)\right]\end{align*}$$

Problem: When some $\lambda_r = 0$ the above expression is ill defined.

Partial summary

Example: Consider the reactions $r_1~:~A \to \emptyset$, $r_2~:~2A \to 3 A$, with

$$\lambda_1(x) = x\,,\qquad \lambda_2(x) = x^2 1_{[0,1]}(x)$$

We see that $X_0=0$ is an absorbing state for $X_t^v$. However for $z(t) = t$

$$I_{0,T}(z) \leq \int_0^1 1 \log \frac 1{\lambda_2(t)} -1 + \lambda_2(t)+ \lambda_1(t) \,d t < \infty $$

We would like to prove an LDP lower bound, but

\(\bullet\) The local change of measure argument used for this type of processes is ill-defined on the boundary.
\(\bullet\) The LDP depends on the microscopic sequence of initial conditions: if $x_0^v \equiv 0$ then $\log \mathbb P_0[X_1^v>0] \equiv - \infty$.

The main result

Theorem (A., Andreis, Patterson, Renger, 2021):

Assume that $\mathcal D$ is compact, and that $x_0^v \in \mathcal D \subset \mathbb R_+^d$ is a sequence of initial conditions such that $\mathbb P_{x_0^v}[d(X_t^v, \partial \mathcal D)> \epsilon ] > 0 \text{ for all } v>0, t>0$ then a pathwise LDP in \(D_{0,T}(\mathcal D)\) holds with rate function $I_{x_0,T}(z)$.

Proof sketch

We estimate $\mathbb P_{x_0^v}[\sup_{t \in [0,T]}\|X_t^v - z(t)\|_2< \epsilon]$ for a fixed $z \in \mathcal{AC}_{0,T}(\mathcal D)$

We approximate the original path $z$ away from the boundary with a $\delta$-perturbation $\mathfrak{z}_\delta$ resulting from a combination of

$\bullet$ an initial shift $\,\,s_{\delta}(t) = x_0 + w\,t $ on $\,\,t \in (0,\delta)$
$\bullet$ a shifted version of the path $z$: $\,\,z_{\delta}(t) = z(t-\delta)+ w\,\delta $ on $t \in (\delta,T)$

Proof sketch

We then show that the LDP holds for the shifted path and that it converges to the desired LDP:

$\bullet$ LDP holds for
$\,\,s_{\delta}(t) = x_0 + w\,t $ on $t \in (0,\delta)$
$\bullet$ LDP holds for $\,\,z_{\delta}(t) = z(t-\delta)+ w\,\delta $ on $t \in (\delta,T)$
$\bullet$ $\lim_{\delta \to 0} I(s_\delta) = 0$
$\bullet$ $\lim_{\delta \to 0} I(z_\delta) = I(z)$

$$ \begin{align*}\Rightarrow P_{x_0^v}[\|X^v - z\|< \epsilon] & \approx \lim_{\delta \to 0} P_{x_0^v}[\|X^v - z_\delta\| < \epsilon/2,\|X^v - s_\delta\| < \epsilon/2] \\& \asymp \lim_{\delta \to 0} e^{-v I(s_\delta)}\cdot e^{-v I(z_\delta)} \asymp e^{-v I(z)}\end{align*}$$

Proof sketch:

  • Consider $A \to 2A$, so that $\Lambda_+(x) = x$, and $\Gamma = \mathcal B_{[0,\delta]}(z, \delta/2)$ for $z(t) = t$
  • Couple $X_t^v$ to $\tilde X_t^v$ adding a ghost reaction with $\Lambda_0 = 1$ $c^{r_0} = 0$, so that
    $$\mathbb P(X_{\delta}^v \in \mathcal B_{\delta/2}(\delta)) = \mathbb P(\tilde X_{\delta}^v \in \mathcal B_{\delta/2}(\delta)) \geq \mathbb P(\text{only} + \text{ jumps}| B_\delta) \mathbb P(B_\delta)$$ for $B_\delta = \{(\text{# jumps before }\delta) \in (v \delta/2, 3v \delta/2)\}$
  • So we can estimate $$ v^{-1}\log \mathbb P(X_{\delta}^v \in \mathcal B_{\delta/2}(\delta)) \geq v^{-1} \sum_x \log \mathbb P_x(\tau_+ < \tau_0| B_\delta) $$ $$\qquad \qquad \quad \approx \int_0^\delta \log \Lambda_+(x) d x$$

Conclusions

  • LDP for processes with vanishing rates can be established, and holds quite generally for mass action systems

Open questions

  • Add spatial diffusion to this model
  • Mass action kinetics at multiple scales
  • Establish positive recurrence based on structural properties of the network
