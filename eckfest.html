Chemical Reaction Networks

Consider the reactions \(\emptyset \overset{r_1}{\rightharpoonup} A + B \overset{r_2}{\rightharpoonup} 2B \overset{r_3}{\rightharpoonup} \emptyset\). We see sets of

  • Molecules \( \ \mathcal S = \{A,B\}\qquad\qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\quad\)
  • Reactions \( \ \mathcal R = \{r_1,r_2,r_3\}\)
  • In/out combinations \( \mathcal C = \{(0,0),(1,1),(0,2)\}\qquad\qquad\)

A chemical reaction network \((\mathcal S, \mathcal C, \mathcal R)\) is defined by the sets

  • \( \ \mathcal S := \{s_1, s_2,\dots, s_d\}\) of chemical species,
  • \( \ \mathcal R := \{r_1, r_2,\dots, r_m\}\) of chemical reactions,
  • \(\ \mathcal C := \bigcup_{r \in \mathcal R} \{c_{\rm in}^r, c_{\rm out}^r\}\) of reaction complexes.$\qquad \qquad \qquad \quad$

Can we find a convenient structural representation of the network allowing us to infer some of its dynamical properties?

Microscopic Markov model

With \(N_t \in \mathbb N_0^d\) counting the molecules of each species in a reactor we model the dynamics of the system through a pure jump Markov model.

Each reaction \(r\) is a jump by \(c^r := c_{\rm out}^r - c_{\rm in}^r\) and occurs at a rate \( \Lambda_{r}\) where

$$\Lambda_r(n):= k_r \prod_{i = 1}^d\binom{n_i}{(c_{\rm in}^r)_i}(c_{\rm in}^r)_i! = k_r \prod_{i = 1}^d n_i^{(c_{\rm in}^r)_i}(1 + o_n(1))$$

Ex: $A +B \to 2B$ is a jump of $(0,2)- (1,1) = (-1,1)$ at rate $ \Lambda(n) = k \cdot n_A \cdot n_B $

Generator of $N_t$: $\mathcal L f(n) := \sum_{r \in \mathcal R} \Lambda_{r} (n) \left(f(n + c^r) -f(n)\right)$

Weakly reversible CRNs

Definition: A chemical reaction network $(\mathcal S, \mathcal C, \mathcal R)$ is called weakly reversible if, when interpreted as a reaction graph (complexes are nodes and reactions are directed edges), all its connected components are strongly connected.

The main result

Theorem (A., Anderson, Cappelletti, Mattingly, 2022+):

Let $(\mathcal S, \mathcal C, \mathcal R)$ be a weakly reversible chemical reaction network and let $d = 2$, then $N_t$ is positive recurrent for any choice of positive rate constants $\{k_r\}_{r \in \mathcal R}$.

Foster-Lyapunov estimates

Sufficient condition for recurrence: there exists Lyapunov function $V(\,\cdot\,) > 1$ and a compact $\mathcal K \subseteq \mathbb N_0^d$ with

$$\mathcal L V(n) < -1 \quad \text{for all } n \not \in \mathcal K$$

We proceed to establish an analogous estimate a) in the bulk and b) on the boundary of the state space separately.

The bulk, aka deterministic regime

In the bulk, i.e., when $n_A, n_B \gg 1$, we approximate to leading order

$$\Lambda_r(x):= k_r \prod_{i = 1}^d\binom{x_i}{(c_{\rm in}^r)_i}(c_{\rm in}^r)_i! \approx k_r \prod_{i = 1}^d x_i^{(c_{\rm in}^r)_i}=: \lambda_r(x)$$

and approximating the difference term by a derivative we obtain

$$\mathcal L V(x) = \sum_{r \in \mathcal R} \Lambda_{r} (x) \left(V(x + c^r) -V(x)\right) \approx \sum_{r \in \mathcal R} \lambda_r(x) c^r \cdot \nabla V(x)$$

Chemical Lyapunov function

We use the chemical Lyapunov function

$$U(x) := \sum_{i=1}^d x_i \log x_i - x_i + 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \nabla_i U(e^{\rho w}) = \rho w_i $$

Writing \(x_i = e^{\rho w_i}\) with \( (w, \rho) \in S^{d-1}\times \mathbb R_{\ge0}\), we wish

$$\frac{{\rm d}}{{\rm d}t} U(x(t)) = \langle \nabla U (x),\frac{{\rm d}}{{\rm d}t} x(t) \rangle = \rho \sum_{r \in \mathcal R} k_r \langle w, c^{r} \rangle e^{\rho \langle w, c_{\rm in}^r\rangle} \overset{!}{ <} 0$$

This holds if for \(\rho\) large enough and any \(w \in S^{d-1}\), the reaction dominating the sum (maximizing \(\langle w, c_{\rm in}^r\rangle\)) contributes negatively to it \((\langle w, c^{r}\rangle < 0)\).

Strongly endotactic networks

The network \((\mathcal S, \mathcal C, \mathcal R)\) is called strongly endotactic if, for all \(w \in S^{1}\), the set of dominant reactions \(\mathcal R_w = \arg\max_{r \in \mathcal R} { \langle w, c_{\rm in}^r\rangle }\)
contains only reactions with \(\langle w , c^{r}\rangle \leq 0~,\)
and at least one has \( \langle w , c^{r}\rangle < 0~.\)

This can be checked algorithmically.

$[$Gopalkrishnan, Miller, Shiu, '16$]$

Averaging in the bulk

It can be shown that weakly reversible networks are strongly endotactic for almost all $w \in S^1$. For some $w \in \mathcal W$ the dominant reactions may have \( \langle w , c^{r}\rangle = 0~.\)

We need to average out these reactions, in order to "see" the contribution of the lower order ones (which plays in our favor).

Transition to boundary behavior

Consider $\emptyset \to A+B\,, \quad 5A + 2 B \to 3B \to 2B\,, \quad 2A + B \to 2A $

$$\Lambda_{\emptyset \to A+B}(n) \approx k \quad \Lambda_{2A+B\to 2A }(n) \approx k' n_A^2{n_B}\quad \Lambda_{5A+2B\to 3B }(n) \approx k'' n_A^5 \binom{n_B}{2}$$

Adapting the proof

We sequentially coarse-grain the fibers and define our Lyapunov function as constant on such coarse-grained sets, relaxing our dissipation condition to $$\mathbb E_{n} \int_{0}^{\tau_i} \mathcal L V(N_s) ds \le -C\cdot \mathbb E_{n}[ \tau_i]\qquad \text{for } N_t \not \in \mathcal K$$

where $N_0 = n$ and $\tau_i$ is the time to exit fiber $i$.

Choosing $V((n_A,0)) = e^{n_A}$ we have for $c_1,c_2 > 0$ $$\mathbb E_{n} \int_{0}^{\tau_i} \mathcal L V(N_s) ds = \mathbb E_{n}V(n_{\tau_i}) - V(n) \leq - e^{n_A} (c_1 \mathbb P_n(-) - c_2 \mathbb P_n(+)) $$ so we are done if, for any $n_B$, $\mathbb P_n(-)/\mathbb P_n(+) \to \infty$ as $n_A \to \infty$.

Final step: contradiction

To prove the desired estimate, choosing $V((n_A,0)) \sim \mathcal O(e^{n_A})$ it is sufficient to show that the process has probability $1$, to leading order, to exit a fiber to the left.

Further we note that the choice of $\{k_r\}$ does not affect the leading order exit probabilities $\mathbb P_n(-),\mathbb P_n(+) $ from fibers.

Theorem (Anderson, Craciun, Kurtz, 2010): Let $(\mathcal S, \mathcal C, \mathcal R)$ be a weakly reversible chemical reaction network, there exist reaction rates $\{k_r\}$ such that the invariant distribution of $N_t$ is Poisson.

The superexponential decay of Poisson tails is in contradiction with a comparably likely exit probability from fibers.
